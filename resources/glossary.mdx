---
title: Glossary
description: "Clear definitions for key terms related to Dexari."
---

## Account
A user profile on Dexari that allows access to platform features, including wallets, trading, and rewards.

## API (application programming interface)
A set of rules and protocols that allows different software applications to communicate with each other.

## Asset
Any digital item of value, such as cryptocurrencies or tokens, that can be held or traded on Dexari.

## Blockchain
A decentralized, digital ledger that records transactions across many computers in a way that prevents alteration.

## Custodial wallet
A wallet where a third party holds and manages your private keys and assets on your behalf. Dexari is a self-custodial platform.

## Decentralized exchange (DEX)
A platform that allows users to trade cryptocurrencies directly with each other without relying on a central authority.

## Digital asset
A broad term for any asset that exists in digital form, including cryptocurrencies, tokens, and NFTs.

## Fiat currency
Government-issued currency, such as US dollars (USD), euros (EUR), or yen (JPY), that is not backed by a physical commodity like gold.

## Gas fee
A transaction fee paid to process and validate operations on a blockchain network, such as Ethereum.

## KYC (know your customer)
A verification process required by financial platforms to confirm the identity of users, often involving personal documents.

## Liquidity
The ease with which an asset can be quickly bought or sold without affecting its price.

## Market order
A type of order to buy or sell an asset immediately at the best available price.

## NFT (non-fungible token)
A unique digital asset that represents ownership of a specific item, artwork, or collectible, and cannot be exchanged on a one-to-one basis with other tokens.

## Non-custodial wallet
A wallet where you control your private keys and assets. Dexari provides a non-custodial wallet, meaning only you have access to your funds.

## Order book
A real-time list of buy and sell orders for a specific asset on a trading platform.

## Passkey
A secure, passwordless authentication method that uses cryptographic keys, often tied to your device or biometrics.

## Perpetual contract (perps)
A type of derivative contract that allows traders to speculate on the price of an asset without an expiration date.

## Private key
A secret code that allows you to access and control your digital assets. Keep your private key secure and never share it.

## Public key
A cryptographic code that allows others to send assets to your wallet address.

## Slippage
The difference between the expected price of a trade and the actual price at which it is executed, often due to market volatility or low liquidity.

## Spot trading
The purchase or sale of a digital asset for immediate delivery and settlement.

## Stablecoin
A type of cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value, often pegged to a fiat currency like the US dollar (e.g., USDC, USDT).

## Staking
Locking up digital assets to support the operations of a blockchain network, often in exchange for rewards.

## Two-factor authentication (2FA)
An extra layer of security requiring two forms of verification before granting access to an account.

## Wallet
A digital tool that stores your cryptocurrencies and tokens, allowing you to send, receive, and manage your assets.

## Web3
A new generation of internet applications that use decentralized networks, such as blockchains, to give users more control over their data and assets.
